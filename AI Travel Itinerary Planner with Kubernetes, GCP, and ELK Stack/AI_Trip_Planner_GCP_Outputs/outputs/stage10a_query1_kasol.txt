Here's a detailed day-wise itinerary for a 3-day trip to Kasol, Himachal Pradesh:

Day 1:
- Morning: Arrive in Kasol and check into a budget-friendly hotel or hostel. Explore the Kasol market and try some local food at a café.
- Afternoon: Take a short hike to Chalal village, which is a scenic and peaceful destination. Visit the Gurudwara Manikaran Sahib in the afternoon.
- Evening: Visit some nice cafes in Kasol, such as the Evergreen Café or the Moonlight Café, and enjoy the scenic views of the Parvati Valley.

Day 2:
- Morning: Start early for the Kheerganga trek, which is a popular trekking route in Kasol. The trek takes around 4-5 hours to complete and offers stunning views of the valley.
- Afternoon: Reach the top of the trek and enjoy the scenic views. Have lunch at a local café or restaurant.
- Evening: Return to Kasol and visit the Kasol market for some shopping and exploration.

Day 3:
- Morning: Visit the village of Tosh, which is known for its scenic views and trekking routes. Take a short hike to the Tosh village and explore the local market.
- Afternoon: Visit the hot springs in Manikaran, which are believed to have healing properties. Try some local food at a café or restaurant.
- Evening: Return to Kasol and enjoy the scenic views of the Parvati Valley. Try some local cuisine at a café or restaurant.

Food and restaurant recommendations:
- Evergreen Café: Known for its scenic views and delicious food.
- Moonlight Café: Offers a variety of cuisine, including Indian, Chinese, and Italian.
- Local dhabas: Try some local food at a dhaba, such as parathas, momos, or thukpa.

Local tips and travel advice:
- Kasol is a budget-friendly destination, and accommodation options are available at affordable prices.
- The best time to visit Kasol is from April to October, when the weather is pleasant and the trekking routes are open.
- Try to avoid visiting Kasol during the peak season, as it can get crowded and expensive.
- Always carry cash, as mobile network services may not be available in some areas.
- Respect the local culture and environment, and try to minimize your impact on the destination.

Estimated costs:
- Accommodation: ₹500-₹1,000 per night for a budget-friendly hotel or hostel.
- Food: ₹200-₹500 per meal for local cuisine.
- Transportation: ₹100-₹500 for local transportation, depending on the mode of transport.
- Trekking: ₹500-₹1,000 per person for a guided trek, depending on the route and duration.

Note: The estimated costs are subject to change and may vary depending on the individual's preferences and exchange rates.
